Shruthi Gowda

PhD Candidate & (Previously) Research Engineer with over 10 years of experience in Computer Vision, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Embedded Software, applied to domains such as Autonomous Driving, Quality Inspection, and Healthcare.

Research Interests: Neuro-inspired Artificial Intelligence, Cognitive Bias, Continual Learning, Model Robustness, Multi-Modality Learning.

Professional Experience

Research Engineer & Tech Lead – NavInfo Europe B.V., Eindhoven, NL (2019–2023)

  • Led design and implementation of the Vision Development Module, a computer vision library for industrial defect detection.
  • Developed algorithms for adversarial robustness and continual learning optimized for edge devices.
  • Contributed to multi-view geometry, camera calibration, and 3D vision projects.

Senior Software Engineer – National Instruments (Vision R&D), Bangalore, IN (2013–2019)

  • Built a high-performance deep learning inference engine by integrating TensorFlow and Intel OpenVINO for NI embedded hardware.
  • Developed and shipped industrial inspection products as part of the NI Machine Vision library, enabling robust quality control.

Research Assistant – Image and Signal Processing, UCL, Belgium (2012–2013)

  • Conducted research on advanced image processing techniques for signal and vision applications.

Projects

SAFEXPLAIN (EU Horizon Project)

As Tech Lead for the automotive use case, I designed safety patterns and developed novel explainable AI components for critical embedded systems. This project ensures that AI-driven features in autonomous vehicles adhere to ISO 26262 Functional Safety standards. Read the Automotive Case Study for details.

ENFIELD (EU Horizon Project)

Within this collaborative effort involving 15 partners, I researched and implemented adaptive artificial intelligence algorithms featuring lifelong learning, robustness, and fairness. The ENFIELD project aims to create trustworthy AI systems for dynamic real-world environments. More information at the ENFIELD project site.

NI Machine Vision Library (National Instruments)

I led the development of a machine vision library that integrates deep learning inference capabilities with traditional computer vision. This product supports industrial defect inspection workflows and embeds AI models on NI hardware. Learn more on the Vision Development Module page.
